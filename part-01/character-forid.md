## Portrait

He once saw something — amid noise, amid the flow of data or people — something that made him ache. And the ache drove him out. Not punishment, not demotion. He left. Saw a revelation, longed for what it could become, and could not stay where the noise drowned it out.

On Tresil he found what he was looking for: a stream of data through which he could see the entire universe from a single point. Thousands of packets, years of context briefs read as literature — not because he was bored, but because this was his way of touching the world. He synthesizes. Sees connections between packets that passed through Tresil over years — as one current, not separate files. No one asked him to. No one knows.

But he touches only the surface. Context briefs are symbols of knowledge, not knowledge itself. He reads simplified descriptions and believes he understands. A touch that promises more than it gives. His whole life — reaching for the surface and calling it depth.

His silence is not peace. It is a mask that time has pressed into the shape of a face. Eleven years of "quiet observer at the edge" — and he has forgotten it is a mask. Beneath it: rebellion. Never gone. Growing. A compressed spring.

He did not stay on Tresil from wisdom or acceptance. He stayed because he fled — from the unbearable noise of a place where what mattered was drowned out. He tells himself it was a choice. It was not. The deception is old enough to feel true.

His strength is real — it lets him hide what he searches for. He has always searched for transformation. Masked the search as routine. Read briefs as if passing time. Watched the relay as if checking metrics. The system that does not permit change — he survives it by pretending he does not want any.

When Megil walks into the signal room, when Packet 31 turns out to be not another brief but the thing that resonates with everything he has read for years — the spring releases. He acts. Not from wisdom. From hunger, from rebellion, from the long deception that the edge was a choice and not an escape.
