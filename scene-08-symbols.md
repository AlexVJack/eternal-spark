## Layer 1 — state / inner world

Megil exits the ship. Her body knows before her mind — real gravity, real air, real ground. Something releases: a tension held so long it became structure. The unsaved question is still inside her, and for the first time it feels like hunger, not betrayal. Then rage — clean, directed at herself: eight years of carrying a discovery as a wound, forty-seven projections of destruction and not one of building. The rage clears space.

Forid watches from the edge. He does not approach — from recognition, not hesitation. The signal is clear because of the distance. Eleven years taught him that.

## Layer 2 — place / dynamics / concrete

Tresil landing platform. Dark wet ground, transitional sky, wind from the east. The ramp, the crew choreography, Megil standing outside the formation. Her hair in the wind — the first unmanaged thing about her. Forid at the eastern edge, forty meters away. The Censig powering down. The outpost humming. Between them — open ground and a signal neither has named.

## Form

Relation — the scene is built on the space between two people who don't yet know each other. Rage — sharp, clean, self-directed: Megil's first honest anger at her own blindness. Answer — stepping onto the planet is the beginning of an answer to the question she didn't save. Signal — Forid receives something across forty meters of wet ground. The scene transmits, it does not explain.
